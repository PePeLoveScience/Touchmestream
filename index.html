What to check

Signs of a clear promotion

Before entering any sweepstakes, look for transparent rules and a real sponsor identity. A legitimate promotion should make it easy to understand who runs it and how winners are selected.

  • Official rules with eligibility, start/end dates, and entry limits.
  • A clear sponsor or promoter name and contact information.
  • Prize details, approximate retail value, winner selection method, and notification process.
  • Privacy information explaining how your data may be used.
  • No claim that every entrant receives a prize or reward.
Safety

Red flags to avoid

Do not continue with any promotion that asks for sensitive credentials or uses pressure tactics that make the offer hard to understand.

  • Requests for passwords, one-time verification codes, or banking PINs.
  • Pressure to pay unexpected fees to receive a prize.
  • No official rules, no privacy details, or no clear sponsor identity.
  • Misleading countdowns, fake scarcity, or language that promises a prize.
  • Requests to tag friends or share to personal timelines as a required entry method.
Responsible participation

What participation means

Entering a sweepstakes is optional. Some promotions are only available in certain countries or to people above a minimum age. Your odds depend on the official rules and number of eligible entries.

  • Only enter if you meet the age and location requirements.
  • Submit accurate information and follow the stated entry limits.
  • Keep a copy of the official rules for reference.
  • Unsubscribe from marketing messages if you no longer want them.
